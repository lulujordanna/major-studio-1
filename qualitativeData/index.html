The Palettes of The Met Gala

A Qualitative Data Visualization

How does the theme of the Met Gala contribute to the visual identity?

The Met Gala, marking the opening of the Costume Institutes annual fashion exhibition has become a cultural phenomenon. Designers and celebrities come together to celebrate the Costume Institute and dress accordingly to the years theme. Looking at the topics of the shows, they are so unique from one another and provoke different thematic responses. With my background in graphic design I was curious as to how the theme contributes to the visual language. I constructed a color analysis of the past five fashion exhibitions and began to question the associations of the color based on the theme and how the exhibitions compare over a period of time. This visualization was built through HTML, CSS, Vibrant JS and D3. I selected images from the exhibition histories and built color palettes based off of the object within the shows. Looking at a five-year time span, I am comparing Camp: Notes on Fashion (2019), Heavenly Bodies: Fashion and the Catholic Imagination (2018), Rei Kawakubo/Comme des Garcons: Art of the In-Between (2017), Manus x Machina: Fashion in an Age of Technology (2016) and China: Through the Looking Glass (2015).

Methodology

This visualization was built through HTML, CSS, Vibrant JS and D3. The data was parsed from The Metropolitan Museum of Arts website based on past exhibitions. The total sample size is 30 objects to build a qualitative comparison over the past five years. All images have been credited to the owners/photographers.
